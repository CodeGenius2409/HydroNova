How HydroNova Works

A complete technical breakdown of the system — from raw source water to AI-controlled distribution.

1 · System Overview
OVERVIEW

HydroNova is a modular, end-to-end smart water treatment platform that converts contaminated source water — surface water, groundwater, or municipal supply — into WHO-grade potable water. The system chains seven physical treatment stages with a real-time AI control layer that monitors 24 sensors per unit at 100 Hz, runs predictive analytics on edge hardware, and continuously tunes pump speeds, dosages, and UV intensity to maximise output and minimise energy use.

2 · The 7-Stage Pipeline
PIPELINE
01 · INTAKE

A submersible pump draws raw water through a stainless-steel mesh strainer (5 mm aperture). A flow meter and turbidity probe at the intake establish a baseline signature for the AI controller before any treatment begins.

02 · SEDIMENT

A multi-media tank — gravel, graded sand, and anthracite — removes suspended particles down to 20 µm. Backwash cycles are triggered automatically when the differential pressure across the bed exceeds 0.4 bar.

03 · FILTRATION

A spiral-wound activated-carbon block followed by a 0.1 µm hollow-fibre ultrafiltration membrane removes chlorine, organics, and 99.99% of bacteria and protozoa. Permeate flux is logged per minute.

04 · UV PURIFICATION

Filtered water passes a 254 nm UV-C reactor delivering ≥40 mJ/cm² — sufficient for 4-log inactivation of viruses including rotavirus and adenovirus. A photodiode confirms lamp output every 10 ms.

05 · AI MONITOR

An on-device neural processor fuses pH, ORP, turbidity, conductivity, free chlorine, temperature, and flow data. A CNN classifies contamination signatures in <12 ms and an LSTM forecasts component wear up to 30 days ahead.

06 · STORAGE

Treated water is buffered in a UV-shielded food-grade tank with continuous residual-chlorine dosing (0.2–0.5 mg/L). Three level probes and a recirculation pump prevent stagnation.

07 · DISTRIBUTION

A variable-frequency-drive pump pressurises the network to 2.5 bar. Acoustic leak sensors along the line stream data back to the AI core for sub-litre/min leak detection and GIS mapping.

3 · AI Control Architecture
NEURAL CORE

The control stack runs three coordinated models on a Cortex-A78 edge SoC: a CNN for contamination classification (200+ classes), an LSTM for time-series prediction of fouling and wear, and a PPO reinforcement-learning agent that adjusts pump speed, valve positions, UV intensity, and dosage every second to maximise reward = (purity × throughput) − (energy + chemical cost). Telemetry is mirrored to the cloud over 4G/LoRaWAN for fleet-wide learning.
